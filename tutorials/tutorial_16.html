Temporal Generalization

Decoding Neural Stability across Time (Voigtlaender et al., 2023)

Test Time ($t_2$) Train Time ($t_1$)
Null / Low (D ≈ 0) High Distinctness (D > 0)
Theoretical Framework: To test if a representation is truly stable or transforming, we compute the Expected Cross-time Information:
\[E_{12} = \sqrt{|D_1 D_2|} \cdot \text{sign}(D_1) \cdot \text{sign}(D_2)\]
Where $D_1, D_2$ are pattern distinctness at $t_1, t_2$ on the diagonal.

Inference Mode

Key Definitions

  • Main Diagonal ($t_1 = t_2$): Measures the instantaneous strength of the representation.
  • Off-Diagonal ($t_1 \neq t_2$): Measures pattern generalization. How much information remains in the same "code"?
  • Dynamic Representation: When off-diagonal info is significantly lower than predicted by the diagonal strength.
