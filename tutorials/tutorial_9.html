cvMANOVA vs. SVM

Why classification accuracy is often the wrong metric for inference.

The "Accuracy" Ceiling

Standard multivariate pattern analysis (MVPA) often uses classification accuracy (e.g., SVM). However, accuracy is non-linear—it saturates at 100%. Once at the ceiling, accuracy cannot distinguish between a strong representation and an even stronger one.

The cvMANOVA Solution

Pattern distinctness ($\hat{D}$) is a continuous, variance-based measure. It provides an unbiased estimate of the true population effect size that does not saturate and remains invariant to the amount of training data.

Key Observations

SVM Limitations

As you increase the Number of Trials, notice how SVM mapping accuracy quickly hits **100%**. At this point, even if you increase the 'True Distance', the accuracy cannot move further. This makes it impossible to compare effect sizes between different brain regions or sessions once data is sufficient.

cvMANOVA Advantage

The cvMANOVA statistic **$\hat{D}$** remains stable as you increase Trials. Because it is an unbiased estimator of the population variance, it is **invariant to the amount of data**. It scales linearly with the signal-to-noise ratio, providing a true measure of representation strength.
