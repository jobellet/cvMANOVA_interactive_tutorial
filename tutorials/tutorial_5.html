Tutorial 05

Why Cross-Validation?

See how standard MANOVA is always positively biased under the null — and how cvMANOVA corrects this.

The Problem: Standard MANOVA estimates the distance between conditions using the same data used to fit the model. Because noise causes random differences between group means, the estimated effect is always > 0, even when there is truly nothing there.

The Solution: Cross-validation measures patterns on held-out data, making the estimator unbiased. Under the null, the distribution centers exactly at 0, and false positive rate is properly controlled.

The Key Insight

Bias Grows with p/df

Increase voxels (p) or decrease error df using the sliders above. Watch MANOVA's null distribution shift further right — the bias explodes.

Negative D̂ is Good News

~50% of cvMANOVA estimates fall below 0 under the null. This is expected and correct — it confirms the estimator is truly unbiased.
