Factorial Designs

Extracting interactions and testing cross-context consistency.

The Problem with Classifiers

Standard decoders are built for binary discrimination. Testing for interactions in factorial designs is cumbersome with classifiers.

cvMANOVA leverages the MGLM, where testing interactions is as simple as defining the appropriate contrast matrix.

Interaction Effects

An interaction tests if the pattern difference for Factor A changes depending on the level of Factor B.

• Integration: Strong non-linear interaction.

• Superimposition: Absence of interaction (linear codes).

The "Stability" Secret

In traditional cross-decoding, you train on one level of Factor B and test on another. In cvMANOVA, Consistency expresses this directly. When Factor A patterns are identical across contexts, the interaction is zero and stability is maximal. This metric quantifies the degree of stability of a pattern across different contexts.
