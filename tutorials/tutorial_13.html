The Power of Bayesian cvMANOVA

Standard frequentist statistics ($p$-values) can only "fail to reject" the null hypothesis. They cannot provide evidence for the null. This makes it impossible to distinguish between a shared supramodal representation and a simple lack of sensitivity.

By pairing cvMANOVA with Bayes Factors, Rizza et al. (2024) demonstrated that:

  • Both visual and auditory maps activate identical frontoparietal circuits.
  • The neural patterns are mathematically indistinguishable (BF < 1).
  • This confirms a truly supramodal spatial code—the brain uses the same coordinate system regardless of how the information was perceived.
