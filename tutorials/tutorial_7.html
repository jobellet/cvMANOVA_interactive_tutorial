cvMANOVA vs. LDC

Why standardization matters: turning raw distances into a valid inferential map.

Both metrics are unbiased estimators under the null hypothesis. The key difference is standardization: LDC (raw): D̂ = cross-validated Mahalanobis distance cvMANOVA: D̂ₛ = D̂ / √p where p is the number of voxels in the searchlight sphere. Dividing by √p removes the dependency on sphere size.

Null Distribution: Raw D̂ (varies with p)

Distributions shift and scale as you change the number of voxels.

Searchlight Edge Effect

Spheres at the brain boundary have fewer voxels — this creates inhomogeneous null variance.

Why this matters for inference: If you threshold a raw D̂ map, brain-edge voxels (smaller p) will appear artificially significant because their null distribution has lower variance. By standardizing with 1/√p, cvMANOVA creates a homogeneous map suitable for group-level statistics.

Summary

LDC (Raw)

Unbiased estimator of Mahalanobis distance. Good for comparing two regions with equal sphere sizes. Cannot be thresholded fairly across the whole brain.

cvMANOVA (D̂ₛ)

Divides by √p to normalize null variance. Enables valid whole-brain statistical maps and group-level inference with a single threshold.
