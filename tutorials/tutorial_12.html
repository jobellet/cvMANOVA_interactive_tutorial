Standardization & Inference

Turning Raw Cross-Validated Distances into Robust Statistical Z-Maps.

While cross-validated distances (like Mahalanobis) are strictly unbiased (centered at zero under the null), their variance is not constant. In fMRI searchlight mapping, spheres near the brain edges contain fewer voxels (\(p\)), causing the null variance to fluctuate across the brain.

cvMANOVA solves this by generalizing the distance to a standardized pattern distinctness framework, producing estimates that behave like Z-scores for easy group-level analysis.

\[ \hat{D}_s = \frac{1}{\sqrt{p}} \hat{D} \]

Why divide by \(\sqrt{p}\)?

In the raw framework, a region with 100 voxels will have a wider null distribution than a region with 10 voxels. This inhomogeneity makes group-level T-tests invalid. By standardizing by \(\sqrt{p}\), cvMANOVA ensures the null variance is approximately 1.0 everywhere.

Note: Both estimators are centered at zero. This ensures that even if you have negative values in your map, they represent valid null fluctuations rather than systematic bias.
