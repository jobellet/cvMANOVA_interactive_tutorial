Information Mapping

Move a spherical searchlight across a synthetic 2D brain and watch D-values emerge.

The searchlight technique moves a small sphere (radius r) through every location in the brain. At each position, cvMANOVA computes pattern distinctness D — yielding a whole-brain information map where hotter colors indicate more decodable representations.

Key Observation

Radius Too Small

Few voxels per sphere → covariance estimate is noisy → D values are highly variable. Signal regions still emerge but noisily.

Radius Too Large

More voxels than df_error → covariance becomes rank-deficient. Spatial specificity is also lost (blurring).
